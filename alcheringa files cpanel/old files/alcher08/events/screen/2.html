Screenplay – Shadow Play

Preliminary round- On the basis of the performance in Preliminary round, 5-6 teams will be selected for the final event.

General Guidelines:

  1. A Screen of approximate size of 12 feet width and 10 feet height will be set up on the stage with white cloth on it.
  2. A LCD projector will be projected on the screen so that anything which comes between the projector and screen gives a shadow on the screen.
  3. Team is supposed to do an act on any story/theme/subject.
  4. Time limit will be a maximum of 15 minutes.
  5. At any instant, not more than 4 people can be there on stage.
  6. However, any no. of people can help backstage (like backstage narration, playing background music etc.)
  7. The use of color slides is allowed.
  8. Mics, on stage, can be allowed if required, however it is advised to use effective backstage narration synonymous with the play that is being enacted on stage.
  9. Participants are advised to use choreography, heavy props etc. to make their shadows distinct and attractive.
  10. All the media files for background music, sound effects or narration should be brought in proper MP3 format in a CD to prevent any discrepancy lately. It is advised to label the music files and CD with your team name and keep a backup of CD with you.
  11. The background narrations or any sort of description should be essentially made in Hindi or English. In case the play is in any other language the theme of the play should be explained in English/Hindi at the end of the play. An extra two minutes time will be provided for the same.
  12. Since, the Shodow play screen is very delicate screen and is supported by stands. Thus participants are requested to take special care while performing on the stage.
  13. Negative point will be awarded for any kind of vulgarity or time exceeding.
  14. Teams are supposed to report half an hour before the event and submit their music at least 15 minutes before the event starts. Also one team member should be present with the sound technician.

JUDGING CRITERIA:

  • Theme - Successful depiction/portrayal of selected theme. - 40%
  • Quality of shadows produced - 30%
  • Innovation and Props used. - 20%
  • Back stage narration. (Use of mikes on stage is discouraged. However it can be as a part of props to depict the theme.)- 10%
