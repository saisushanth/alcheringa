Wordsworth – Literary Challenge

Scrabble

  • Four teams (comprising 2-3 players), shortlisted after prelims, compete by forming words from the individual lettered tiles and the clues given.
  • The words would be seen on a square grid, being projected on a screen.
  • For each question, any team can answer by pressing the buzzer.
  • After 10 seconds, letters of the word would start appearing on the screen.
  • Guessing the word after 10 seconds would fetch partial points.
  • There is negative marking for incorrect answer.

Impromptu:

  • This event involves speaking on the spur of the moment.
  • Topics will be given on the spot.
  • Speakers are encouraged to explore the humorous side of the topic.
  • Time limit is 2 minutes. Negative points will be awarded for exceeding the time limit.
  • Participation is limited, and registration is on first come basis.
  • Judging criteria:
    • 40% content
    • 40% presentation
    • 20% innovation

    Turncoat

    • An event in which you score points for your skills of self-contradiction.
    • Participants have to speak on a topic which will be released 3 weeks before the event on our website www.alcheringa.org.
    • The participants will be given 4+1 minutes to talk - minimum 4 minutes and not exceeding 5 minutes, inculcating 2 minutes in favour, and 2 minutes against the topic.
    • Judging Criteria:
      • 30%: Content
      • 30%: Presentation
      • 40%: Ease with which you can change your sides.

    Literary Quiz:

    • 4 Teams (each comprising 2-3 players) fight it out in 4 grueling rounds of quizzing.
    • There are 4 rounds: General, Audio/Visuals, Anagrams, Dumb charades.
    • Anagrams round involves guessing names of famous writers/novels from partially completed words.
    • In case the team for which the question was intended, fails to answer in the stipulated time, the question is up for grabs for the remaining teams.
    • For an incorrect answer, there would be negative marking.

    Reel Writing:

    • Put your creativity to the test.
    • You’ll be shown a short movie, taking its reference and your own ideas, write a lucid essay on the scenario/theme illustrated in the movie.
    • Time limit is 1 hour
