ALCHERINGA 2015

With a history of bringing Guwahati and the North-east into the spotlight, Alcheringa starts afresh for its 19th edition. Known for its fervent exhibition of art and culture, Alcheringa has played expert host to a variety of eminent artistes like the Michael Angelo Batio Swiss Folk Metal band Eluveitie, the Israeli Oriental Metal band Orphaned Land, Irish Folk Band Ciorras, classical maestros like Guilliano Modarelli and Anoushka Shankar, and of course Bollywood personalities such as Mohit Chauhan, Shankar-Ehsaan-Loy, Shilpa Rao, KK, Shaan and Sonu Nigam, to name a few.
Witnessing a staggering attendance of over 50,000 people and 350 colleges over the 4-day fest, Alcheringa is hands-down, one of the biggest events in the Eastern region of the nation. The year 2015 promises to be bigger in all respects. So hold your breath and stays tuned, for the countdown to the "Eternal Dreamtime" begins now!

ALCHERINGA ATLANTIS

The North-east witnesses a colossal fest on the onset of spring each year. 2014 was no different. Alcheringa scaled itself up, and raised the bar higher with this edition. Themed as "Atlantis" this year, the fest drew huge crowds, exceeding 50,000 from the North-east and beyond. Guwahati witnessed scintillating performances from the legendary Michael Angelo Batio and Undying Inc, thrilling metal purists of the region. The highlight of the Bollywood night, Crescendo was a mesmerising performance from Shilpa Rao - who wooed the crowd with an amazing performance. Villalobos Brothers from Mexico highlighted "Saaz", the Classical night and pretty much knocked the crowd out!
Alcheringa Atlantis also saw the maiden edition of the IITG Model United Nations. Headbangers got their piece with a three day long Rock-o-Phonix, and the crowd revelled at the astounding performances in Electric heels, and the jaw dropping models participating in Haute Couture and Femina Miss India (judged by former Miss India Earth - Miss Shobhitha Dhulipala). The 4 day long fest marked one of the most happening dates of the North-east, and aims at getting bigger and better with each coming edition.
